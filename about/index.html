About

I am a modeling specialist with more than twenty-five years of experience in tire mechanics, multi-physics finite element analysis, thermodynamics, and durability modeling. My work integrates operator-based thinking, nonlinear solver design, and large-deformation elastomer mechanics. Recently I have been developing a unified FEA framework built on multi-level Augmented Lagrange methods and the Rᵀ–L–S modeling language.

My research interests include: structural stiffness architecture in complex composites, rolling resistance and dissipation modeling, viscoelastic constitutive theory, operator-theoretic PDE structures, solver admissibility, and the interplay between mathematical modeling and engineering design.

Personal

I divide my time between technical writing, poetry, and research in continuum mechanics. Much of my creative work explores the connection between memory, landscape, and structure — whether in engineering, literature, or daily life. I was born in China, and have spent many years in Akron, Ohio, whose winters and quiet evenings appear frequently in my poems.
