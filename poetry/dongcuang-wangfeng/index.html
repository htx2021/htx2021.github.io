冬窗望枫

冻云噙雨过疏枝，谁把晶珠缀墨丝？

半壁横斜凝泪影，一窗澄澈挂冰姿。

偶从枯寂窥灵动，渐信空无孕妙奇。

正是微光初渡处，万千星子欲垂时。

Watching Maples Through a Winter Window

Frozen clouds, rain held fast, pass sparse branches—
who strung crystal beads along threads of ink?
Across half the wall, slanted shadows congeal like tears;
within one window, clarity suspends a poise of ice.

From barrenness, I chance upon a glimpse of life,
and come to trust: emptiness can bear the marvelous.
So let me gather all things in a glassbound realm,
and wait for dawnlight to discern what once had been.

深冬雨后，窗外高枫黑枝交错，晶珠悬挂其上。久视之，目光渐被雨珠牵引，枝影退为线条。

After a winter rain, black branches of a tall maple cross outside my office window, beads of water hanging from them. With time, the eye begins to follow the droplets first, while the branches recede into mere lines.
